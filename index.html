YAN Mandate

The Youth Advocate Network (YAN) unites and equips young leaders to champion inclusive and sustainable development in Rwanda.

Established in 2024 under Rwandan law, the Youth Advocate Network (YAN) is more than just an organization; it is a vital umbrella for a diverse array of youth‑led initiatives, movements, NGOs, and associations.

Headquartered in Kigali, YAN serves as a central hub connecting passionate young advocates across Rwanda and extending influence regionally and globally through collective action.

Vision & Mission

Vision

Building a Rwanda where young people are empowered, actively engaged, and leading transformative advocacy for inclusive and sustainable development.

Mission

To equip and mobilize young advocates with the knowledge, skills, and platforms to champion child rights, social inclusion, and policy change, ensuring meaningful youth participation in national and global development agendas.

About Youth Advocates Network

Youth Advocates Network (YAN) is a youth-led umbrella platform that connects and empowers young organizations to influence inclusive development.

Youth Advocates Network (YAN) is a youth-led and youth-serving network that empowers young people to amplify their voices and meaningfully participate in decision‑making spaces.

Born from UNICEF Rwanda’s Youth Advocacy Guide (YAG) training, YAN connects youth leaders with advocacy, communication, and stakeholder engagement skills for shared action.

Strategic Orientation

YAN strengthens youth voice, collaboration, and advocacy through coordinated capacity building, skills transfer, and collective action.

  • Strengthening youth voice and agency in societal issues.
  • Promoting solidarity and collaboration among youth‑led organizations.
  • Facilitating capacity building, advocacy, and community‑level impact through collective action.
  • Encouraging sustainable, youth‑driven development and social behavior change across sectors.

Context & Rationale

Rwanda’s youth-led ecosystem is vibrant but fragmented, and many organizations still face structural and sustainability capacity gaps.

Rwanda has a vibrant but fragmented ecosystem of youth‑led organizations across health, education, social justice, climate, and inclusion. Many groups still face structural challenges in project management, governance, and sustainability systems.

YAN responds by building collective capacity, coordination, and advocacy power so youth organizations can sustain impact despite shifts in funding, policy, and community demand.
